## Access billing settings

Open billing settings from your organization dashboard.

<Steps>

<Step title="Open settings">

Go to **Settings**.

</Step>

<Step title="Select organization settings">

Choose **Organization Settings** for the organization you want to manage.

</Step>

<Step title="Open billing">

Click **Billing** to view your plan, usage, payment, and subscription controls.

</Step>

</Steps>

Only organization admins should manage billing details, payment methods, credit packs, overage settings, and paid add-ons.

## Subscription overview

The billing page shows your current subscription and the limits attached to your organization.

### Current plan details

Your subscription overview includes:

- Current plan type, such as Starter, Standard, Professional, or Enterprise
- Billing cycle, such as monthly or yearly
- Next billing or reset date
- Editor seat limit and current editor usage
- Documentation site limit
- Monthly AI credit allocation
- Active credit pack, when one is configured
- Overage status and overage limit
- Active paid add-ons, such as remove-branding

Current plan pricing for new paid subscriptions is:

| Plan | Monthly | Yearly equivalent | Monthly credits | Editor seats | Documentation sites |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| Standard | $69/month | $55/month billed yearly | 10,000 | 3 | 2 |
| Professional | $199/month | $159/month billed yearly | 30,000 | 10 | 4 |
| Enterprise | Custom | Custom | Custom | Custom | Custom |

Starter is free and includes 1 editor seat and 1 documentation site. New Starter organizations receive 10,000 trial credits for 14 days, but Starter does not include monthly plan credits after the trial ends.

<Callout kind="info" collapsed="false">

Legacy subscriptions may keep older prices, credit allocations, or seat limits. The billing dashboard is the source of truth for the limits that apply to your organization.

</Callout>

## Paid add-ons

Paid add-ons extend your organization without changing the base subscription plan.

### Remove-branding add-on

The remove-branding add-on enables white-label branding controls for your documentation sites. When the add-on is active, site settings include a per-site control for showing or hiding Documentation.AI branding.

This setting is applied per documentation site. Use it to keep Documentation.AI branding visible on internal docs while hiding it on customer-facing docs, or to apply different branding requirements across multiple sites.

<Callout kind="info" collapsed="false">

Purchasing, removing, or changing the remove-branding add-on requires an organization admin because it changes billing. Site-level branding visibility can be managed by users who have access to that documentation site's settings.


</Callout>

## Credit packs and overage

Credit packs and overage help paid teams continue using AI after the included monthly credits are exhausted.

### Credit packs

Credit packs add a recurring monthly pool of credits. Documentation.AI consumes plan credits first, then pack credits.

Available pack sizes are 5k, 10k, 20k, 30k, 50k, and 100k credits per month. Pack prices appear in Stripe checkout.

- Upgrading a pack grants additional credits immediately and charges the prorated difference.
- Downgrading a pack keeps the current pack credits until the next renewal cycle.
- Removing a pack leaves remaining pack credits available until depleted.

### Overage

Overage is disabled by default. When enabled, Documentation.AI continues AI requests after plan and pack credits run out and bills usage at **$0.01 per credit**.

You can set an optional monthly USD limit. When the limit is reached, new AI requests are blocked until the next reset or until the limit changes.

<Callout kind="alert" collapsed="false">

Overage requires an active Stripe subscription with a payment method. If overage is disabled and credits run out, AI features stop accepting new requests until credits reset or more credits are added.

</Callout>

## Payment methods

Manage payment methods through Stripe from the billing page.

### Supported payment options

- Credit cards, including major card networks
- Debit cards
- Bank transfers for eligible Enterprise agreements

To update payment information, open the billing dashboard and launch the Stripe Customer Portal. Stripe stores and manages payment method details.

## Billing history and invoices

Use the billing dashboard or Stripe Customer Portal to review invoices and payment history.

Each invoice can include:

- Billing period and invoice date
- Plan subscription charges
- Paid add-on charges, such as remove-branding
- Credit pack charges
- Overage charges, when enabled and used
- Taxes, when applicable
- Payment method used

If you need invoice details for accounting or procurement, download the invoice from Stripe or contact support with the invoice number.

## Subscription changes

### Plan upgrades

If your organization already has an active Stripe subscription with a saved payment method, Documentation.AI can upgrade the plan in place without opening a new checkout session. Otherwise, the upgrade flow opens Stripe Checkout.

When the plan type changes, Documentation.AI applies the new plan allocation, resets overage used for the cycle, and clears Starter trial credits if a trial was active.

### Plan downgrades

Before downgrading, confirm that your organization fits within the lower plan’s editor seat, documentation site, and monthly credit limits. Features that are not included on the lower plan may become unavailable, but your documentation content remains preserved.

### Cancellations

Cancellations are handled through the Stripe Customer Portal. When Stripe marks a subscription as ended, Documentation.AI reverts the organization to Starter.

## Failed payments and past-due status

If a payment fails, Stripe retries the payment during its dunning window. A subscription in `past_due` status stays on its current plan while Stripe retries payment.

Documentation.AI reverts an organization to Starter only when Stripe marks the subscription as ended, such as `canceled`, `unpaid`, or `incomplete_expired`.

## Tax information

Taxes are calculated through Stripe based on billing details and location. Depending on your region and account type, invoices may include VAT, sales tax, or other applicable taxes.

If your organization has a tax ID, VAT number, or tax-exempt status, update those details in Stripe from the billing dashboard.

## Troubleshooting

<ExpandableGroup>

<Expandable title="My payment failed">

Open the Stripe Customer Portal from billing settings and update your payment method. If the payment is still declined, contact your bank to confirm the charge is allowed.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="My subscription is past due">

Stripe is retrying the payment. Your current plan remains active during the retry window. Update your payment method before Stripe marks the subscription as unpaid or canceled.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="My credit balance looks wrong">

Check whether your organization has an active credit pack, overage usage, Starter trial credits, or legacy plan limits. If the dashboard still looks incorrect, contact support with your organization name and billing email.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="I need a custom invoice or procurement support">

Contact sales for Enterprise billing support, custom terms, or procurement requirements.

</Expandable>

</ExpandableGroup>
