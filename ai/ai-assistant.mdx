## Overview

The AI Assistant helps readers, customers, and internal teams get fast, accurate answers based on your published documentation. Readers can open **Ask AI** directly, hand off a search query to the assistant from the search modal, or start from the starter questions you configure. The assistant searches your docs, reasons over the content it finds, and returns grounded answers with citations.

Unlike a generic chatbot, the assistant is:

- **Docs-aware** – it relies on your published documentation as its source of truth.

- **Agentic** – it plans how to answer, not just reply turn-by-turn.

- **Cited** – it shows where each answer came from in your docs.

<Callout kind="info">
  The AI Assistant (Ask AI) is separate from the AI Documentation Agent. The **Assistant** answers questions for end users and internal teams.
  The **Documentation Agent** helps you write and edit content. See [AI Documentation Agent](/ai/ai-documentation-agent) for details.
</Callout>

## More than just AI Search – it’s Agentic

Most chatbots:

- Don’t know your docs structure

- Freely improvise when they’re unsure

- Make it hard to see where an answer came from

The AI Assistant:

- **Retrieves first, then answers** – it searches your docs and uses the retrieved content as grounding.

- **Plans actions, not just replies** – it reasons about which docs to consult, when to ask follow-up questions, and how to structure multi-step answers.

- **Stays within your content** – it is designed to prefer your docs over general model knowledge.

- **Signals uncertainty** – when it can’t find reliable content, it avoids making confident but unsupported claims.

This makes it safer to use in support and customer-facing workflows.

## Citations: how they work and why they matter

Every answer from the assistant can include **citations** back to your documentation.

What users see:

- A short answer and explanation in the chat

- One or more citations linked to specific pages (often down to the section)

- The ability to click through to read the full context

Why this is important:

- **Trust** – users can verify information against your canonical docs.

- **Reusability** – support and account teams can safely reuse answers with links in tickets, chats, or emails.

- **Maintainability** – updating your docs improves future answers automatically; you can trace bad answers back to specific content.

If an answer cannot be strongly supported by what’s in your docs, the assistant will either:

- Ask for clarification, or

- Indicate that it doesn’t have enough information, instead of fabricating details.

## Where users access the assistant

The assistant appears in your documentation as an **Ask AI** entry point. You can keep the assistant closed until a reader opens it, or configure it to open automatically when the page loads.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Ask AI">

  Readers open the assistant from the Ask AI entry point in your documentation. If `ai-assistant:default-open` is enabled, the assistant opens automatically on page load.

  </Step>

  <Step title="Ask a question or choose a starter question">

  Readers can type a question in natural language. Starter questions give them suggested prompts before they write their first message, which helps guide common support and onboarding flows.

  </Step>

  <Step title="Review the grounded answer">

  The assistant searches your docs, reasons over the results, and responds with a concise answer, optional step-by-step guidance, and citations to relevant pages or sections.

  </Step>
</Steps>

## Search handoff

The search modal can hand a reader's query to Ask AI when regular search results are not enough. After a reader types a search query, the modal can show an **Ask AI assistant** option.

When the reader selects that option, Documentation.AI closes the search modal, opens the assistant, and sends the search text as the first message. If an assistant response is already running, the current run is reset before the new search query is submitted.

<Callout kind="info">
  The search handoff only appears when the assistant is enabled and the reader has entered a non-empty search query.
</Callout>

## Configure the assistant experience

Use the dashboard AI settings or the `ai` section in `documentation.json` to control how Ask AI appears on your deployed site.

- **Enable or disable Ask AI** with `ai-assistant:enabled`. The assistant is enabled by default when this setting is omitted, but you can turn it off without contacting support.

- **Open Ask AI automatically** with `ai-assistant:default-open`. The assistant starts closed by default; set this to `true` for docs that rely on conversational onboarding or support.

- **Add starter questions** with `ai-assistant:starter-questions`. Keep starter questions short, specific, and written in the reader's language, such as `How do I import existing docs?`.

- **Choose the assistant style** with `ai-assistant:style`. Use `default` for the template's built-in assistant panel, or `floating` for a chat window anchored near the bottom-right of the page on desktop.

- **Adjust floating placement** with `ai-assistant:position`. Use `default` for the standard floating position or `bottom-spaced` to move the floating assistant higher above the bottom edge, which helps avoid overlap with cookie banners or support widgets.

- **Protect Ask AI with captcha** using `ai-assistant:captcha-enabled`. When this setting is `true` and hCaptcha is configured for your deployment, Ask AI runs an invisible anti-bot check before sending a question.

<Callout kind="tip">
  Captcha protection usually does not add a visible checkbox. Most readers will only notice it if hCaptcha decides to show an additional challenge.
</Callout>

## Benefits for admins and doc owners

The AI Assistant can:

- **Improve onboarding** – new users reach first value faster by asking concrete “how do I…” questions.

- **Reduce support tickets** – many basic “where is…” and “how do I…” questions are handled directly in the docs.

- **Make customer-facing teams faster** – support and AM/CS teams can use the same assistant to pull accurate, cited answers during conversations.

- **Expose documentation gaps** – repeated questions without good citations highlight where your docs need better coverage or structure.
